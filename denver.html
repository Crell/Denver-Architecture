Drupal site considerations

Is this site for...

A small church

  • Low-traffic
  • Anonymous traffic
  • Dozens of pages
  • Mostly static content
  • Learnability
  • Expediency
  • Low-memory
  • Scalability
  • Verifiability

A major media network

  • Hundreds of hits / second
  • Lots of social networking features
  • Millions of pages
  • Constantly changing content
  • Scalability
  • Fast-to-run
  • Verifiability
  • Expediency

Is this site going to be mostly...

Anonymous

  • Varnish solves all most
  • Simpler caching strategy
  • Rarely reach Drupal
  • Fast-to-run
  • Low-memory
  • Scalability

Authenticated

  • Varnish is no help
  • Complex caching strategy
  • Every line of PHP matters
  • Fast-to-run
  • Low-memory
  • Scalability
  • Expediency

You can't have your cake and eat it too

You can't have your cake and eat it too

  • Modifiability vs. Performance. Cost of abstraction
  • Expediency vs. Modifiability: "Doing it right" takes time
  • Extensibility vs. Testability: Hooks & bare data structures
  • Extensibility vs. Understandability: "Wait, what all is happening?" (preprocess?)
  • Verifiability vs. Expediency: Go learn Erlang or Haskel

Complementary design

  • Testability and Modifiability both require similar abstraction
  • Testable code is inherently modular
  • The easiest way to make something extensible is to make it modular
  • A well-designed system is rapidly extensible. Expedient later, not now.

Testability and Modifiability are complementary

Complementary design

  • Performance: seconds / request
  • Scalability: requests / second
  • Fewer seconds/request => more requests/second

Better Performance usually improves Scalability
